My mother ran a tailoring shop out of our front room, and I grew up under the table, sorting buttons by size. By eleven I was stitching blouse linings myself — badly, at first, seams all crooked. She never redid my work in front of me. She'd wait until I was asleep, unpick it quietly, and hand it back the next morning like nothing had happened.

I have my own shop now, two streets from where hers used to stand. The machine is newer, but I still keep a tin of mismatched buttons under the counter, the same way she did.
