Everyone tells me to sell the letterpress and buy a digital printer. It would be faster, they say, cheaper per card. Maybe. But when a customer runs a finger over a wedding invitation and feels the letters pressed into the paper, they always pause. That pause is why I kept it.

My father bought the machine secondhand in 1979. I was seven when he first let me set a single line of type, my hands too small for the composing stick. I got the spacing wrong. He printed it anyway and pinned it above the door, where it still hangs.
