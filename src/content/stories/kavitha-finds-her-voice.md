Growing up, singing was something I did for other people — at weddings, at school assemblies, at my grandmother's request every Friday evening. It never occurred to me that I could sing for myself until a friend recorded me humming while we waited for a bus and sent it back to me later that night.

I listened to it on loop, embarrassed at first, then something else. I signed up for classes the next week. I was twenty-six.
