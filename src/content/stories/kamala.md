I have been stitching clothes in this small shop for over thirty years now. My mother taught me on an old Singer machine when I was just twelve — long before I ever learned to read a full sentence in school.



Every morning, I open the shutters at seven and the first customers are usually schoolchildren needing a quick hem before class. By evening, it's mostly saris for weddings, each one a small story of its own.



People ask me if I get bored doing the same work for so long. I tell them — every piece of cloth is different, every person who wears it is different. That's enough newness for one lifetime.
