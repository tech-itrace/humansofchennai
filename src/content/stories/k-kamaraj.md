I never finished school myself — I had to leave early to help support my family. Maybe that is why, when I later had the chance to shape policy, the one thing I insisted on was that no child should ever miss a meal, or miss school because of hunger.

I served as Chief Minister of Madras State for a decade, and in that time we opened new schools, built new colleges, and pushed hard on rural roads and industry. But the midday meal scheme is the decision people still remember — because it kept children in classrooms instead of pulling them out to work.

Later in life, I was known more for the leaders I helped bring forward than for holding office myself. Some called me a "Kingmaker." I never minded that title. Power was never the point — building something that outlasted me was.
