He grew up in a house on a quiet street in Kodambakkam, in a family of session musicians, surrounded from childhood by the sound of film orchestras rehearsing. His father, R. K. Shekhar, composed and conducted for Malayalam films and died when the boy was nine — the family rented out his father's musical instruments to get by. By his early teens he was playing keyboard on film sessions himself, dropping out of school to tour with an orchestra led by the flautist Sivamani and cellist genius Ilaiyaraaja's group.

## From jingles to Roja

Long before three-letter initials became one of the most recognized names in film music, A. S. Dileep Kumar — the name he was born with — was scoring advertising jingles out of a home studio in Kodambakkam that he built himself, piece by piece, and named Panchathan Record Inn. In 1989 he took the name Allah Rakha Rahman. Three years later, in 1992, the director Mani Ratnam gave him his first film: *Roja*. The score didn't just launch a career — Time magazine would later place it on a list of the ten best soundtracks of all time.

## Madras never left the music

What followed was two decades at the center of Indian film music — *Bombay*, *Dil Se*, *Lagaan*, *Rang De Basanti* — built almost entirely out of that same Kodambakkam studio, expanded over the years but never relocated. Even after Broadway (*Bombay Dreams*, with Andrew Lloyd Webber) and Hollywood came looking for him, Panchathan Record Inn stayed the base of operations. Chennai's film industry likes to claim him as its own not out of sentiment but because it's literally true: nearly everything he has made was made there.

## Jai Ho

In 2009, *Slumdog Millionaire* took his work to an audience that had never heard of Kollywood. He won two Academy Awards in one night — Best Original Score and Best Original Song, for "Jai Ho" — along with a Grammy, a BAFTA, and a Golden Globe. It was the first time an Indian composer had won an Oscar. He accepted it the way he accepted most things in public: quietly, crediting his mother, and thanking Chennai.
