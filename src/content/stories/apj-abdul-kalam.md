I grew up watching birds over the Rameswaram coastline and wondering how anything so heavy as an aircraft could ever leave the ground. That question never really left me — it just changed shape as I grew older, from birds to rockets.

Much of my working life was spent in and around Chennai, in the labs and testing grounds of the country's early missile and space programs. Progress was slow, often frustrating, and failure was common before anything ever flew correctly. But every setback taught us something the next attempt needed.

Later, I spent as much time with students as I did with scientists. Young people would ask me what they should become. I always told them the same thing — dream first, work relentlessly second, and never let your circumstances decide the size of your ambition.
