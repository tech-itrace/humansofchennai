I never set out to build a company the way business schools teach you to. I studied engineering, went abroad, and could have stayed there comfortably. Instead, I came back — first to Chennai, then further out into small towns — because I believed good technology work didn't need to be chained to one city.

Zoho grew slowly and stubbornly, without outside investors telling us what to build next. We built our own products, hired people who never went to elite colleges, and trained them ourselves. Some of our best engineers today started in villages with no formal computer science degree at all.

People ask me why I moved a technology company's engineering culture into rural Tamil Nadu instead of scaling up in a metro. My answer is simple: talent is everywhere, opportunity is not. If we can fix that gap even a little, in even one district at a time, that is work worth doing.
